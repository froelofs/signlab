Datasets

Global SignBank

Together with a team of developers at Radboud University in Nijmegen, we maintain and further develop the Global Signbank platform, which hosts online lexicons for many sign languages around the world. The platform was initially developed by Radboud University in Nijmegen. Main responsibility was transferred to SignLab Amsterdam in 2023.

The NGT lexicon

We also maintain the NGT lexicon on Global Signbank, which was initially developed by Radboud University as well. We have added thousands of signs as well as detailed information about the form of the signs (phonological transcriptions and motion capture recordings), their possible interpretations (multiple senses and links to the Open Multilingual Wordnet), and their usage (example sentences). We are still in the process of adding more signs and more information.

Experimental data

Besides this large, general-purpose dataset, we are also collecting experimental data on specific constructions in NGT and other sign languages (French, Italian, German, Catalan, Turkish, Russian, and Filipino sign languages). We make these datasets available to the research community via our OSF repository.
