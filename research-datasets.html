Datasets

Together with a team of developers at Radboud University in Nijmegen, we maintain and further develop the Global Signbank platform, which hosts online lexicons for many sign languages around the world. The platform was initially developed by Radboud University in Nijmegen. Main responsibility was transferred to SignLab Amsterdam in 2023.

We are also substantially extending the NGT lexicon on Global Signbank, which was initially developed by Radboud University as well. We are adding thousands of signs as well as detailed information about the form of each sign (phonological transcriptions and motion capture recordings), the possible interpretations of each sign (multiple senses and links to the Open Multilingual Wordnet), and the usage of each sign (example sentences).

Besides this large, general-purpose dataset, we are also collecting experimental data on specific constructions in NGT and other sign languages (French, Italian, German, Catalan, Turkish, Russian, and Filipino sign languages).

We make the datasets that we produce available to the research community via our OSF repository.
